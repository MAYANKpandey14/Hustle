# India's Geopolitical Stance in APAC and IOR (2024–2026)

India's geopolitical strategy for the 2024–2026 period marks a significant transition from "strategic autonomy" to a more proactive **"multi-alignment"** stance. India is increasingly positioning itself as a **"net security provider"** in the Indian Ocean Region (IOR) and a **"leading power"** that bridges the gap between the Global North and the Global South.

## Strategic Frameworks & Doctrines

### SAGAR & MAHASAGAR

- **SAGAR (Security and Growth for All in the Region):** India's foundational maritime doctrine, focusing on cooperative security and economic growth.
- **MAHASAGAR (Mutual and Holistic Advancement for Security and Growth Across Regions):** An evolution of SAGAR (introduced 2024-2025) that emphasizes collective maritime security and holistic economic cooperation with IOR littoral states like Sri Lanka, Maldives, Mauritius, and Seychelles.

### Act East Policy (AEP)

The AEP celebrated its 10th anniversary in 2024. It has shifted from purely economic engagement to deep strategic and defense partnerships with ASEAN, Japan, and South Korea.

### Indo-Pacific Oceans Initiative (IPOI)

A key pillar for India’s APAC engagement, focusing on seven pillars including maritime security, maritime ecology, and resource management.

## Regional Engagement & Security

### Maritime Assertiveness in the IOR

India has intensified its role as a regional stabilizer:

- **Chokepoint Monitoring:** Maintaining a permanent naval presence at key chokepoints like the Strait of Malacca and Bab el-Mandeb.
- **Anti-Piracy Operations:** High-profile rescues (e.g., MV Abdullah) and constant patrolling to ensure the safety of Sea Lines of Communication (SLOCs).
- **Information Fusion Centre (IFC-IOR):** Serving as a regional hub for maritime domain awareness.

### Asia-Pacific (APAC) & ASEAN

- **ASEAN Centrality:** India continues to support ASEAN centrality while deepening "Comprehensive Strategic Partnerships" with Singapore and Vietnam.
- **Trusted Supply Chains:** Focus on semiconductors, AI, and cybersecurity to build supply chains resilient to Chinese influence.

## Multi-Alignment and Global Leadership

### The Quad (India, US, Japan, Australia)

- **Summits:** India hosted the Quad Leaders' Summit in 2024 and is projected to host it again in 2026.
- **MAITRI (Maritime Initiative for Training in the Indo-Pacific):** A key initiative focused on capacity building for regional partners.

### Bridge Between Blocs

India occupies a unique "geopolitical sweet spot," engaging with Western democracies through the Quad and G7 (as an invitee) while maintaining a lead role in the **BRICS** (hosting the summit in 2026) and the **Global South**.

## Key Strategic Pillars

| Pillar                 | Focus Area              | Key Initiatives                                    |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Maritime Security**  | Net Security Provider   | MILAN & Malabar exercises, IFC-IOR.                |
| **Economic Diplomacy** | Supply Chain Resilience | IPEF, Bilateral FTAs (UAE, UK, EU).                |
| **Tech & Defense**     | Indigenization & iCET   | Co-production of GE-F414 engines, Predator drones. |
| **Global South**       | Leadership & Advocacy   | Voice of Global South Summits.                     |

## Challenges & Risks

1. **Chinese "Encirclement":** Countering the "String of Pearls" strategy and China's growing footprint in the South China Sea.
2. **Neighborhood Instability:** Managing shifting political landscapes in the Maldives, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.
3. **Economic Asymmetry:** Addressing the significant trade deficit with China while striving for "Atmanirbhar" (self-reliance).

## Conclusion

By 2026, India aims to consolidate its role as a global "stabilizer," leveraging its economic momentum and multi-aligned diplomacy to shape a rules-based order in the Indo-Pacific.
