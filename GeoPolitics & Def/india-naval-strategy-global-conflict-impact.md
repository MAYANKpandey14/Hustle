# India's Naval Strategy and Global Conflict Impact (2024–2026)

## Executive Summary

India is transitioning from a regional "net security provider" to a global **"Preferred Security Partner."** This shift is underpinned by the **MAHASAGAR** doctrine and a strategic network of naval bases. Concurrently, global conflicts (Red Sea, Russia-Ukraine, Middle East) have disrupted supply chains, leading to "imported inflation" that is significantly straining the Indian middle class through stagnant wages and rising costs of living.

## Naval Presence in the IOR

### 1. Strategic Doctrines: SAGAR to MAHASAGAR

- **MAHASAGAR (2025):** An evolution of the 2015 SAGAR policy, focusing on *Mutual and Holistic Advancement for Security and Growth Across Regions*. It emphasizes collective security with Global South partners (Africa and SE Asia) rather than unilateral dominance.
- **"First Responder" Role:** The Indian Navy has institutionalized "Mission-Based Deployments" (MBD), keeping 14–15 warships permanently stationed at key chokepoints like the Strait of Malacca and the Bab el-Mandeb.

### 2. Territorial Security & The "Reverse String of Pearls"

To counter Chinese expansion, India has fortified its own "strategic anchors":

- **INS Jatayu (Minicoy, Lakshadweep):** Commissioned in March 2024, this base monitors the **Eight Degree Channel**, a critical maritime artery, and serves as a vital counterweight to regional shifts in the Maldives.
- **Agalega Island (Mauritius):** In 2024, India operationalized a 3,000-meter airstrip and jetty, enabling long-range surveillance by **P-8I Poseidon** aircraft across the Southwest Indian Ocean.
- **Duqm (Oman) & Sabang (Indonesia):** India has secured long-term logistical access to these ports, extending its reach to the Western and Eastern edges of the IOR respectively.

### 3. Supply Chain Maritime Statecraft

- **Pax Silica Initiative (2026):** India joined a US-led coalition to secure the "silicon stack." The Navy now protects trade routes specifically for **critical minerals** (lithium, cobalt) and semiconductor components.
- **Subsea Cable Protection:** With 95% of India's digital traffic moving through undersea cables in the IOR, the Navy has intensified patrolling around these "silent" supply chains.

## Impact of Global Conflicts on Trade

### 1. The Red Sea Crisis & Logistics (2024–2025)

- **Rerouting Costs:** Houthi attacks forced ~80% of India-Europe container traffic to bypass the Suez Canal for the Cape of Good Hope, adding **12–15 days** to transit and increasing freight costs by **40–60%**.
- **Export Vulnerability:** Low-margin sectors like textiles, furniture, and leather have become less competitive globally due to "war premiums" on shipping.

### 2. Energy and Agriculture Disruptions

- **Imported Inflation:** India's 85% dependency on imported crude oil makes it hyper-vulnerable to Middle East tensions. While discounted Russian oil provided a buffer, the risk to the **Strait of Hormuz** (40% of India's oil flow) has kept domestic fuel prices high.
- **Fertilizer Squeeze:** Conflict-led disruptions in the supply of **Potash and Phosphates** from the Middle East and Ukraine have raised cultivation costs, leading to food inflation.

## Impact on the Indian Middle Class

The "Global Polycrisis" has created a significant financial squeeze on roughly 40 million tax-paying middle-class households.

### 1. The "Cost of Living" Squeeze

- **Essential Inflation:** While headline CPI hovered around 5%, "essential inflation" (food, fuel, healthcare, and education) has been much higher. Private education costs are rising at **10–12% annually**.
- **Imported Tech Costs:** Reliance on China for electronic components means global trade wars directly translate to higher prices for smartphones, laptops, and home appliances.

### 2. Stagnant Wages & Job Insecurity

- **The IT Slowdown:** The IT sector faced a hiring freeze due to global recession fears and **AI disruption**. In 2025, 86% of Indian professionals reported workplace disruption due to AI.
- **Real Wage Growth:** For many middle-management roles, real wage growth has been near zero since 2024.

### 3. The "K-Shaped" Consumption Pattern

- **Luxury vs. Mass-Market:** record sales for premium SUVs and luxury apartments contrast with sluggish sales for entry-level cars and FMCG.
- **Rising Debt:** Household debt-to-income ratios (excluding home loans) rose to **33–34%** in 2025.
