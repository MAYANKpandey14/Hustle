# India’s Indo-Pacific Strategy: From Declaratory Ambition to Durable Influence

India’s Indo-Pacific strategy has evolved from a cautious diplomatic framing into a layered regional approach built around ASEAN centrality, SAGAR, IPOI, maritime partnerships, and selective minilateralism rather than formal alliance politics. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)

The core test is now execution: whether New Delhi can translate this vision into sustained influence through naval capacity, economic delivery, institutional coherence, and credible presence across the Indian Ocean and onward to Southeast Asia and the wider Pacific. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)

## Executive Summary (TL;DR)

- India’s official Indo-Pacific vision took clearer shape in Prime Minister Modi’s 2018 Shangri-La Dialogue speech and was institutionalized through the creation of an MEA Indo-Pacific Division in 2019 and the launch of the Indo-Pacific Oceans Initiative the same year. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)
- New Delhi’s preferred framing is “free, open, inclusive,” rules-based, and anchored in ASEAN centrality; it explicitly avoids presenting the Indo-Pacific as an anti-China bloc even while expanding partnerships with the US, Japan, Australia, ASEAN states, and Indian Ocean littorals. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)
- Maritime security remains the hard core of the strategy because Indian trade, energy access, sea-lane security, anti-piracy operations, and regional crisis response all depend on sustained naval and coast guard reach across the Indian Ocean and adjoining chokepoints. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/blog/indias-evolving-maritime-strategy-in-the-indo-pacific-region)
- China is the principal structural driver behind India’s Indo-Pacific turn, but India has so far chosen a mix of limited hard balancing, soft balancing, capacity-building, and coalition-building rather than alliance alignment or direct containment. [carnegieendowment](https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2017/09/indias-strategic-choices-china-and-the-balance-of-power-in-asia)
- ASEAN, the Quad, SAGAR, and IPOI are not interchangeable tools: ASEAN provides legitimacy and regional architecture, the Quad delivers targeted public goods and maritime awareness, SAGAR supplies India’s Indian Ocean doctrine, and IPOI is the umbrella for issue-based cooperation. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)
- India’s biggest constraints are internal: naval modernization delays, submarine procurement drift, infrastructure slippages, and fragmented execution across ministries and policy frameworks. [businessstandard.substack](https://businessstandard.substack.com/p/indias-naval-push-goes-slow-amid)
- Success will be judged less by speeches and more by whether India can remain the most reliable first responder, security partner, development actor, and standards-setting power in its near seas while expanding practical influence eastward into Southeast Asia. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/blog/indias-evolving-maritime-strategy-in-the-indo-pacific-region)

## Context & Background

### Facts

India was initially slower than some partners in formally embracing the Indo-Pacific concept, but its maritime outlook had earlier roots in the Malabar exercise, post-2004 tsunami cooperation, and the gradual strategic extension of Act East into maritime Asia. [frstrategie](https://www.frstrategie.org/en/publications/notes/india-s-indo-pacific-origins-trajectories-and-circulation-concept-2025)\
\
The conceptual breakthrough came with Modi’s June 2018 Shangri-La address, which described the Indo-Pacific as a “natural region,” stressed inclusiveness, freedom of navigation, peaceful dispute resolution, and ASEAN centrality, and rejected the idea that India saw the region as a closed club directed against any country. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)\
\
In April 2019, the MEA created a dedicated Indo-Pacific Division, and in November 2019 India launched IPOI with seven pillars: maritime security, maritime ecology, maritime resources, capacity building and resource sharing, disaster risk reduction and management, science and technology and academic cooperation, and trade connectivity and maritime transport. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)

### Analysis

This evolution shows that India did not adopt the Indo-Pacific as a borrowed slogan; it adapted the concept to preserve strategic autonomy while widening its theater of action from the eastern coast of Africa to the western Pacific. [rusi](https://www.rusi.org/explore-our-research/publications/policy-briefs/indo-pacific-indian-foreign-policy)\
\
The resulting doctrine is deliberately dual-use: it reassures Southeast Asia through inclusive language while signaling to major powers that India intends to shape rules, connectivity standards, and maritime order. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)

### Uncertainties

India’s official rhetoric still leaves room for multiple interpretations, especially on how far New Delhi is prepared to move from “inclusive balancing” toward sharper strategic competition if Chinese pressure intensifies. [airuniversity.af](http://airuniversity.af)\
\
That ambiguity has diplomatic value, but it can also blur priorities and slow operationalization.

## Geopolitical Drivers

### Facts

India’s official Indo-Pacific vision rests on sovereignty, territorial integrity, freedom of navigation and overflight, unimpeded lawful commerce, peaceful resolution of disputes, and a rules-based order. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)\
\
Modi’s 2018 speech linked the Indian Ocean to India’s future, described the Malacca Strait and South China Sea as vital connectors to major partners, and argued that sea lanes must remain pathways to prosperity rather than arenas of coercion. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)\
\
The MEA stated in 2025 that India continues to deepen defense and maritime-security cooperation, supported by economic and developmental assistance, and highlighted Quad initiatives such as the Indo-Pacific Partnership for Maritime Domain Awareness, MAITRI, the Indo-Pacific Logistics Network pilot, and the Quad Ports of the Future Partnership. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)

### Analysis

The strategic logic is straightforward: the Indo-Pacific matters to India because maritime order now connects deterrence, trade, technology, energy security, crisis response, and diplomatic leverage. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)\
\
India’s approach therefore combines three layers, the Indian Ocean as the priority theater, Southeast Asia as the legitimacy hinge, and the wider Pacific as the arena where coalition politics shape the balance with China. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)

### Uncertainties

The degree of future US commitment, ASEAN cohesion, and regional appetite for coalition-building remains uncertain, which means India cannot base its strategy on external balancing alone. [warontherocks](https://warontherocks.com/india-and-the-rise-of-china-soft-balancing-strategy-reconsidered/)\
\
This is why self-strengthening and issue-based partnerships matter more than slogans about a “free and open” region.

## India Lens

### Maritime security imperatives

India’s own official articulation ties maritime security to trade, energy, connectivity, HADR, anti-piracy, and blue-economy cooperation. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)\
\
The Indian Navy’s 2024 anti-piracy and rescue operations in the Indian Ocean, including the rescue of 23 Pakistani nationals from a hijacked vessel, reinforced India’s claim to be a first responder and net security provider in the region. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/blog/indias-evolving-maritime-strategy-in-the-indo-pacific-region)\
\
SAGAR, first articulated in 2015 and repeatedly linked to Indo-Pacific policy thereafter, remains the clearest expression of India’s Indian Ocean doctrine. [rusi](https://www.rusi.org/explore-our-research/publications/policy-briefs/indo-pacific-indian-foreign-policy)

### China’s challenge and India’s balancing options

China is the main structural challenge behind India’s Indo-Pacific turn, not only because of military asymmetry but also because Beijing’s influence in Asia and multilateral institutions can constrain Indian interests. [carnegieendowment](https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2017/09/indias-strategic-choices-china-and-the-balance-of-power-in-asia)\
\
Scholarly assessments cited in the available sources describe India’s current response as a mix of internal balancing, regional partnerships, soft balancing, and limited hard balancing rather than a full alliance strategy. [airuniversity.af](http://airuniversity.af)\
\
India’s own official language remains careful: Modi said the Indo-Pacific should not be seen as directed against any country, while also insisting on rules, open commons, and sustainable connectivity standards that implicitly push back against coercive behavior. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)

### ASEAN, Quad, SAGAR, and IPOI

ASEAN is central to India’s Indo-Pacific diplomacy both rhetorically and institutionally; the MEA states that India’s vision is premised on ASEAN centrality, and the Shangri-La speech placed Southeast Asia at the center of the regional architecture. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)\
\
The Quad is more operational than ideological in India’s current framing, focused on maritime awareness, training, logistics, ports, and public goods rather than collective defense. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)\
\
SAGAR gives India’s strategy its Indian Ocean anchor, while IPOI provides a modular platform for cooperation across maritime security, ecology, disaster management, technology, and connectivity. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)

### Analysis

India’s optimal posture is not binary choice between nonalignment and alliance; it is layered balancing that preserves decision-making freedom while raising the costs of unilateral Chinese dominance. [carnegieendowment](https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2017/09/indias-strategic-choices-china-and-the-balance-of-power-in-asia)\
\
ASEAN gives India regional legitimacy, the Quad gives it capability multipliers, SAGAR gives it geographic prioritization, and IPOI gives it a practical agenda. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)\
\
If used coherently, these instruments can help India avoid the trap of overdependence on any one partner while still shaping the strategic environment.

### Uncertainties

ASEAN’s internal divisions, varying threat perceptions, and sensitivity to bloc politics limit how far it can align with India on security questions. [warontherocks](https://warontherocks.com/india-and-the-rise-of-china-soft-balancing-strategy-reconsidered/)\
\
The Quad’s future effectiveness will depend on delivery, especially whether announced initiatives create visible benefits for regional states rather than remaining elite-level declarations. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)

## Economic Statecraft, Supply Chains, and Connectivity

### Facts

India’s official Indo-Pacific approach includes trade connectivity and maritime transport as one of IPOI’s seven pillars. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)\
\
Modi’s 2018 speech argued that connectivity must rest on sovereignty, transparency, consultation, good governance, viability, and sustainability, and should empower states rather than trap them in impossible debt. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)\
\
The MEA’s 2025 parliamentary answer linked India’s regional strategy not only to defense cooperation but also to economic and developmental assistance, and noted India’s participation with Australia in the Indo-Pacific Economic Framework for Prosperity. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)\
\
The MEA brief also records India’s long effort to build ASEAN connectivity through the India-Myanmar-Thailand Trilateral Highway and a USD 1 billion line of credit for ASEAN connectivity and infrastructure announced in 2015. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)

### Analysis

India’s economic statecraft remains the underpowered leg of its Indo-Pacific strategy.\
\
New Delhi’s normative critique of unsustainable connectivity is strategically sound, but influence will depend on whether India can finance, build, and maintain projects faster and more reliably than it has so far. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)\
\
On supply chains, India has an opening because many regional states want diversification without total decoupling from China; however, India still lacks the scale and implementation speed to become the default alternative on its own. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)

### Uncertainties

The sources available here show India’s intent more clearly than they show measurable outcomes on trade integration, supply-chain relocation, or connectivity completion. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)\
\
That gap itself is analytically important: declaratory ambition has outpaced delivery metrics.

## Internal Security & Systemic Risks

### Facts

India’s coastal and maritime-security architecture was strengthened after the 2008 Mumbai attacks through layered coordination involving the Navy, Coast Guard, radar networks, and maritime operations systems. [theacademic](https://theacademic.in/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/59-1.pdf)\
\
At the external-facing level, India now runs multiple Indo-Pacific mechanisms at once, including Act East, ASEAN-led forums, IORA, SAGAR, IPOI, Quad initiatives, and bilateral maritime partnerships. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)

### Analysis

India’s main internal weakness is not lack of strategic language; it is fragmented execution.\
\
Different ministries and agencies own different pieces of maritime security, trade, connectivity, technology, and neighborhood policy, which often produces slow decision cycles, diluted accountability, and uneven follow-through. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)\
\
This fragmentation is especially costly in the Indo-Pacific because influence depends on repeat presence, rapid approvals, and project reliability rather than one-off diplomacy.

### Uncertainties

The available official sources do not fully reveal the internal decision bottlenecks between MEA, MoD, Navy, commerce, finance, and line ministries.\
\
But the pattern of overlapping frameworks and delayed military infrastructure strongly suggests that coordination and implementation remain suboptimal. [indiatracker](https://www.indiatracker.in/story/indias-naval-push-slows-as-project-delays-weigh-on-maritime-ambitions)

## Defence Capability & Gaps

### Facts

A December 2025 CAG performance audit on “Works Management in the Indian Navy,” cited in 2026 reporting, found that 13 of 14 major contracts reviewed were delayed by up to 52 months, with some civil works delayed up to 64 months. [businessstandard.substack](https://businessstandard.substack.com/p/indias-naval-push-goes-slow-amid)\
\
Reporting citing parliamentary data states that naval expenditure rose from Rs 49,623 crore in 2020-21 to Rs 1,03,547 crore in 2025-26, and the Navy’s share of the defense budget increased from 15 percent to 21 percent over that period. [indiatracker](https://www.indiatracker.in/story/indias-naval-push-slows-as-project-delays-weigh-on-maritime-ambitions)\
\
Even so, major modernization programs continue to move slowly; reporting on Project 75(I) says the conventional-submarine program has suffered decades of delay and still lacked a signed contract as of April 2026. [m.thewire](https://m.thewire.in/article/government/as-rajnath-singh-lands-in-germany-two-decades-old-submarine-project-still-awaits-deal)

### Analysis

India has recognized the maritime problem, but budgetary intent has not yet translated into timely force generation. [m.thewire](https://m.thewire.in/article/government/as-rajnath-singh-lands-in-germany-two-decades-old-submarine-project-still-awaits-deal)\
\
The most serious strategic risk is underwater imbalance: if submarine acquisition, maintenance infrastructure, shipbuilding throughput, and maritime ISR integration continue to lag, India’s influence will remain rhetorically expansive but operationally shallow. [businessstandard.substack](https://businessstandard.substack.com/p/indias-naval-push-goes-slow-amid)\
\
A second risk is that civil-works delays and procurement drift erode readiness even when headline spending rises. [indiatracker](https://www.indiatracker.in/story/indias-naval-push-slows-as-project-delays-weigh-on-maritime-ambitions)

### Uncertainties

The sources available here do not provide a full classified picture of force readiness, ammunition depth, or wartime sustainability.\
\
But open-source evidence is sufficient to conclude that procurement speed and infrastructure execution are now strategic variables, not merely administrative problems. [m.thewire](https://m.thewire.in/article/government/as-rajnath-singh-lands-in-germany-two-decades-old-submarine-project-still-awaits-deal)

### Triggers to watch

- More Indian naval and coast guard first-responder operations would strengthen India’s credibility as a net security provider. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/blog/indias-evolving-maritime-strategy-in-the-indo-pacific-region)
- Tangible implementation of Quad initiatives such as IPMDA, MAITRI, and logistics cooperation would indicate movement from rhetoric to public-goods delivery. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)
- Further slippage in naval works, submarine procurement, or infrastructure timelines would be a direct warning sign that execution remains the weak link. [businessstandard.substack](https://businessstandard.substack.com/p/indias-naval-push-goes-slow-amid)

## Strategic Recommendations

### Diplomacy

- Keep ASEAN centrality as the public anchor of India’s Indo-Pacific diplomacy, but move from abstract support to country-specific partnerships with Indonesia, Vietnam, Singapore, the Philippines, and key mainland Southeast Asian states where capacity-building can be delivered quickly. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)
- Use IPOI as a project-management platform, not just a diplomatic label, by assigning lead ministries, timelines, and partner countries for each pillar. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)
- Preserve strategic autonomy, but define clearer red lines on coercion, maritime intimidation, and connectivity practices that undermine sovereignty and debt sustainability. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)

### Military posture

- Prioritize sea denial and maritime ISR in the eastern Indian Ocean and key approaches to the Malacca chokepoint before pursuing wider symbolic presence.
- Fast-track submarine decisions, fleet support infrastructure, repair capacity, and basing resilience, because procurement delay is now a strategic liability. [m.thewire](https://m.thewire.in/article/government/as-rajnath-singh-lands-in-germany-two-decades-old-submarine-project-still-awaits-deal)
- Institutionalize a standing regional HADR and maritime-security package that combines Navy, Coast Guard, airlift, medical support, and digital domain awareness for littoral partners. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/blog/indias-evolving-maritime-strategy-in-the-indo-pacific-region)

### Technology

- Tie Quad and bilateral cooperation to concrete outcomes in maritime domain awareness, secure communications, seabed awareness, unmanned systems, and cyber-resilient port infrastructure. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)
- Build a maritime technology coalition with trusted partners where India contributes software, data fusion, space-enabled monitoring, and training even when hard-platform production remains slower than desired.
- Treat logistics, maintenance, and digital integration as force multipliers equal to platform acquisition.

### Trade and connectivity

- Align connectivity diplomacy with the sovereignty, transparency, viability, and sustainability principles India already states publicly, but pair those principles with faster financing and implementation mechanisms. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)
- Use IPEF participation, ASEAN economic ties, and Indian Ocean partnerships to position India as a diversification node in selected supply chains rather than trying to substitute for China across the board. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)
- Concentrate resources on a small number of visible, finishable corridors and port-linked projects instead of dispersing attention across too many announcements.

### Regional partnerships

- Build concentric circles of partnership: Indian Ocean island states and littorals first, maritime Southeast Asia second, and wider Pacific coalitions third. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)
- Connect SAGAR and Quad delivery by ensuring that regional states see Indian leadership, not only multilateral branding. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)
- Expand training, maintenance support, white-shipping cooperation, and officer exchanges because these create durable influence at lower cost than prestige projects. [theacademic](https://theacademic.in/wp-content/uploads/2024/10/59-1.pdf)

## Key Data Points / Signals to Watch

- Implementation status of IPOI pillars, especially maritime security, disaster management, and trade connectivity. [cuts-global](https://cuts-global.org/pdf/briefing-paper-5-Indias-indo-pacific-strategy-strengthening-partnerships-and-navigating-challenges.pdf)
- Operationalization of Quad initiatives such as IPMDA, MAITRI, the logistics network pilot, and ports cooperation. [mea.gov](http://mea.gov)
- Tempo of Indian anti-piracy, escort, and first-responder deployments in the IOR. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/blog/indias-evolving-maritime-strategy-in-the-indo-pacific-region)
- Movement on Project 75(I) and other submarine or major fleet-acquisition programs. [m.thewire](https://m.thewire.in/article/government/as-rajnath-singh-lands-in-germany-two-decades-old-submarine-project-still-awaits-deal)
- CAG and parliamentary findings on naval infrastructure completion and procurement timelines. [indiatracker](https://www.indiatracker.in/story/indias-naval-push-slows-as-project-delays-weigh-on-maritime-ambitions)
- Whether ASEAN states increasingly seek India for practical maritime capacity building rather than only diplomatic engagement. [drishtiias](https://www.drishtiias.com/daily-updates/daily-news-editorials/strengthening-india-s-indo-pacific-strategy)
